What's Covered

  • Hospitalization expenses (room, ICU, nursing)

Frequently Asked Questions

Common queries about health insurance

What is the waiting period for pre-existing diseases?

Most health insurance plans have a waiting period of 2-4 years for pre-existing diseases. This means any condition you had before buying the policy will only be covered after this waiting period. Some plans offer reduced waiting periods at higher premiums.

Can I get coverage for my parents who are senior citizens?

Yes, we offer special health insurance plans for senior citizens. While the premiums are higher due to increased health risks, these plans provide comprehensive coverage tailored to the needs of elderly individuals, including higher sum insured options and coverage for age-related conditions.

What is the difference between individual and family floater plans?

An individual plan covers only one person, while a family floater plan covers the entire family (typically spouse, children, and sometimes parents) under a single sum insured. Family floaters are generally more cost-effective but the coverage is shared among all members.

Are maternity expenses covered in health insurance?

Maternity coverage varies by plan. Some comprehensive health insurance policies include maternity benefits after a waiting period (usually 2-4 years), while others offer it as an optional add-on. There are also specific maternity plans available.

Can I port my existing health insurance policy to InsureNow?

Yes, you can port your existing health insurance policy to another insurer including InsureNow without losing the benefits you've already accrued (like waiting period credits). The process must be initiated before your current policy's renewal date.
